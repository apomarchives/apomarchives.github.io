ATTORNEY'S FEDERAL-GRADE EVIDENCE TOOLKIT

Developed by a cybersecurity professional with federal investigation experience, this toolkit gives family law attorneys the documentation structure to collect, organize, and present digital evidence that survives judicial scrutiny — including evidence intended for FBI referral or federal submission.

Most digital evidence presented in family court fails not because it doesn't exist, but because it wasn't collected or documented to evidentiary standards. This toolkit fixes that.

WHO THIS IS FOR: Family law attorneys, advocates, and legal professionals handling cases involving cyberstalking, coordinated harassment, digital manipulation, witness intimidation, or any situation where digital behavior needs to become court-ready evidence.

WHAT'S INCLUDED:

  • Evidence collection checklists for social media, messaging, and email platforms
  • Chain of custody documentation templates
  • Metadata preservation standards and verification procedures
  • Evidence organization structure for federal submission
  • Platform-specific screenshot and export protocols
  • Gap analysis worksheet to identify missing evidence
  • Attorney briefing template for client evidence intake
  • RICO pattern documentation framework
